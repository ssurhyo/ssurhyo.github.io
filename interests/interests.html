Personal Interests & Hobbies

Beyond my academic and professional pursuits, I'm passionate about a variety of topics ranging from technology to sports. Here's a glimpse into what fascinates me outside the classroom and workplace.

Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning

My interest in AI and machine learning began during my early university years around 2021, well before the recent explosion of advanced large language models. I was initially drawn to the mathematical foundations of AI, particularly linear algebra and neural networks.

After taking CS540 (Introduction to AI), my fascination with this field has only deepened. I'm especially interested in understanding the inner workings of deep learning systems and how they can be applied to solve complex real-world problems.

The recent advancements in generative AI and large language models have been particularly exciting to follow, as they showcase the remarkable potential of machine learning technologies.

UW-Madison AI Institute Kaggle Competitions
Financial Markets & Quantitative Finance

I've developed a growing interest in financial markets, investment strategies, and quantitative finance. I'm fascinated by the intersection of mathematics, computer science, and economics in modern financial systems.

I enjoy reading about historical market events like the 1929 and 2008 crashes, which provide valuable lessons about market psychology and systemic risks. I'm also interested in learning about investment banking and the evolution of financial institutions.

I follow the careers and investment philosophies of financial luminaries such as Warren Buffett, Jamie Dimon (JPMorgan Chase CEO), and the legacy of Solomon Brothers in shaping modern investment banking.
